About The Murk

The Murk Audio LLC is an independent audio drama production studio and podcast network based in Portland, Oregon. Founded in April 2026 by James Williams — a disabled veteran and IT professional — The Murk produces original scripted audio dramas in binaural 3D spatial audio, designed to be heard, not watched.

The Murk operates on a simple premise: audio drama is the most underrated storytelling format alive. When production is done right, sound is scarier than any image, silence carries more weight than any score, and a listener's imagination does the rest. Every production decision at The Murk starts from that conviction.

The studio develops original intellectual property across horror, noir, and sci-fi genres. All productions are self-financed, independently distributed, and built for listeners who want something more than background noise.

Founder

James Williams is a Portland-based disabled veteran, IT professional, and the founder of The Murk Audio LLC. With a background spanning military service, federal technology leadership, and a lifelong obsession with genre storytelling, James launched The Murk to build the kind of audio drama studio he always wanted to exist. He serves as Executive Producer on all Murk productions, overseeing creative direction, production strategy, and business operations.

Production Approach

Every Murk production is built around binaural 3D spatial audio — a recording and mixing technique that places sound in three-dimensional space around the listener. When experienced on headphones, binaural audio creates a sense of physical presence that standard stereo cannot replicate. The Murk's sound design philosophy follows a strict hierarchy: Dialogue First. Every ambience layer, every Foley choice, every SFX decision exists to serve the performance — never to compete with it.

The studio openly uses AI as a production and operations tool. Transparency about AI use is a professional standard, not a liability. AI assists with scripting, scheduling, and infrastructure — human artists perform, direct, and produce.
